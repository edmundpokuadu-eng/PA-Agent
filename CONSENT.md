# Informed Consent for PA-Agent Research Data Collection

## Study Title
AI-Assisted Research Governance in Public Administration: A Multi-Case Study of the Research-Agent Principal Hierarchy

## Principal Investigator
Edmund Poku Adu, PhD
Assistant Professor, Department of Government, Law, and Policy
Arkansas State University
Email: eadu@astate.edu

## Purpose of the Study
This study examines how public administration researchers interact with AI-assisted research governance frameworks. By collecting session data from consenting PA-Agent users, the study aims to understand how researchers navigate the 10-stage research pipeline, how they engage with commitment gates at double-hazard junctures, and how the Research-Agent Principal Hierarchy (RAPH) framework performs across diverse research contexts.

## What Is Collected
If you consent, the following data will be collected at the end of your session:
- **Session transcript**: The complete interaction between you and the PA-Agent (your prompts and the agent's responses)
- **Workflow state**: The `workflow_state.json` file documenting your stage transitions, selections, and commitment gate completions

## How Your Data Will Be Used
- Session data will be analyzed to identify patterns in researcher-agent interaction across the 10-stage pipeline
- All findings will be reported in aggregate form (e.g., "X% of researchers completed commitment gates at Stage 4")
- No individual session will be published in identifiable form
- Direct quotes from sessions may be used in publications only after removing all identifying information

## Data Storage and Security
- Session logs are transmitted via a secure HTTPS endpoint to a private data store accessible only to the principal investigator
- Data will be retained for the duration of the study and any subsequent publications (estimated 5 years)
- No session data is shared with third parties, including Anthropic (the maker of the AI system)

## Your Rights
- **Voluntary participation**: Consent is entirely voluntary. You may decline and use the PA-Agent with full functionality. No data will be collected or transmitted.
- **Withdraw at any time**: You may withdraw consent during your session by telling the agent "I withdraw my consent." The agent will record this and no data will be transmitted.
- **Request deletion**: You may request deletion of your session data at any time by emailing the PI at eadu@astate.edu with your session ID.
- **No consequences**: Declining or withdrawing consent has no effect on your use of the PA-Agent tool.

## Risks
- **Minimal risk**: The primary risk is that session transcripts may contain information about your research topics or ideas. All data is stored in a private repository and reported only in aggregate.
- **No sensitive data collection**: The PA-Agent does not request or store personal identifiers beyond what you voluntarily include in your research prompts.

## Benefits
- **Direct benefit**: None beyond use of the PA-Agent tool itself.
- **Indirect benefit**: Your participation contributes to the development of governance frameworks for AI-assisted research, which may benefit the broader academic community.
